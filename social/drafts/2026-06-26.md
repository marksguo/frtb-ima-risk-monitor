Weekly FRTB IMA Risk Desk Recap | Week Ending June 26, 2026

Today's 97.5% Expected Shortfall stands at 0.0174. This is the baseline, but the stressed and liquidity-adjusted numbers tell a more complete story. Stressed ES is 0.0256, and liquidity-adjusted ES, which adds extra holding-period penalties for assets that are harder to sell quickly, is 0.0359. The growing gap between these figures highlights the increasing illiquidity premium in the current environment.

We remain in a stressed volatility regime. This implies a higher probability of extreme tail events and warrants continued vigilance in our risk monitoring. The market is pricing in significant downside, and our models reflect that.

The biggest mover today was EEM, down 1.133%. This move contributed materially to the overall ES increase, reflecting ongoing concerns in emerging markets. We are observing how these regional pressures translate into broader portfolio risk.

The weekly model backtest failed, with an Acerbi-Szekely statistic of -0.6659 and 1 VaR exception. This indicates the model may be underestimating risk in certain scenarios, particularly given the current stressed regime. We will be reviewing the underlying assumptions and data inputs.

Looking ahead, I am curious to see how the liquidity-adjusted ES continues to diverge from the stressed ES. This metric will be key in understanding market participants' true perception of asset saleability.
