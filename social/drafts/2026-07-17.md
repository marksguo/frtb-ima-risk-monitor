Weekly FRTB IMA Risk Desk Recap | Week Ending July 17, 2026

Today's 97.5% Expected Shortfall stands at 0.0174, or 1.74%. The stressed regime ES is 0.0256, and the liquidity-adjusted ES (which adds extra holding-period penalties for assets that are harder to sell quickly) is 0.0359. The significant gap between the current and stressed numbers, and then again to the liquidity-adjusted figure, suggests that while current market conditions are relatively calm, a future stressed environment combined with illiquidity could significantly amplify potential losses.

The current volatility regime remains elevated. This implies that while the immediate market risk might appear contained, underlying market dynamics are still unsettled, warranting continued vigilance. Elevated volatility can quickly translate into higher capital requirements under FRTB, so this is a key metric to monitor.

The biggest mover today was EEM, down 1.402%. This emerging markets ETF's movement is a notable contributor to the overall ES calculation, reflecting broader sentiment shifts in riskier assets.

The weekly model backtest produced a FAIL, with an Acerbi-Szekely statistic of -0.6659. Interestingly, there were 0 VaR exceptions. This result indicates that while the VaR component of the model is holding up, the Expected Shortfall estimate itself may be underestimating tail risk, particularly given the elevated volatility regime. This will require further investigation.

Looking ahead, I am curious to see how the model reacts if the volatility regime shifts from elevated to a more neutral or even low state. This could significantly alter the capital charge and risk profile.
