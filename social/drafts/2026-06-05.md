Weekly FRTB IMA Risk Desk Recap | Week Ending June 05, 2026

The 97.5% ES came in at 1.70% today. That number alone looks manageable, but the stressed ES at 2.54% and liquidity-adjusted ES at 3.51% tell a more complete story. The roughly 100bp gap between stressed and current ES reflects how much heavier the tail gets when you recalibrate to a period of genuine market dislocation, and the additional lift to 3.51% once liquidity horizons are applied means the all-in capital charge is more than double the headline figure. That spread is worth watching in weeks where the regime is already flagged stressed, as it is now.

The stressed regime designation is not a surprise given where vol has been sitting, but it keeps the multipliers elevated and limits any near-term relief in capital consumption.

EEM dropping 6.527% was the single biggest mover today. EM equity drawdowns of that magnitude tend to pull correlated positions across the book, so the ES figures above likely understate the diversification drag if other EM exposures moved in sympathy.

The backtest came back as a fail. One VaR exception on its own sits at the edge of the green zone, but the Acerbi-Szekely statistic at -0.4114 is the more informative signal here, suggesting the model is undershooting tail severity even when it technically captures breach frequency.

Forward look: if EEM weakness extends into next week, I want to see whether stressed ES tracks proportionally or whether the model starts clipping the tail.
