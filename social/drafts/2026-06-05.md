Weekly FRTB IMA Risk Desk Recap | Week Ending June 05, 2026

The 97.5% ES came in at 1.70% today. That number alone looks manageable, but the stressed ES at 2.54% and the liquidity-adjusted ES at 3.51% tell a more complete story. The 101 basis point gap between stressed and liquidity-adjusted reflects the additional holding-period penalties for positions in assets that are harder to exit quickly, and in a stressed regime that gap tends to bite harder than it looks on a calm day.

The model is running in a stressed volatility regime, which means the capital multipliers are pulling from the higher-volatility historical window. That keeps ES from being understated during drawdowns, but it also means any relief rally this week would not immediately rotate us back to a normal regime reading.

EEM was the biggest mover, down 6.527% on the day. Emerging market equity selling of that magnitude tends to ripple into correlated positions, so any desk running cross-regional EM exposure alongside EEM would have seen compounding drawdown pressure today.

The weekly backtest came back a fail. The Acerbi-Szekely statistic of -0.4114 with 1 VaR exception is not a multi-exception blowup, but a negative A-S statistic alongside even a single exception suggests the model is underestimating tail severity, not just frequency.

Forward look: if EM selling continues into next week, the liquidity-adjusted ES could widen further as bid-ask spreads on harder-to-exit positions compress available exit windows.
