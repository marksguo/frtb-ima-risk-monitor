Weekly FRTB IMA Risk Desk Recap | Week Ending June 05, 2026

Today's 97.5% ES came in at 1.69%, which looks manageable in isolation. The stressed ES at 2.53% is already 49% wider, and once liquidity horizons are applied the figure jumps to 3.49%, roughly double the base read. That 180 basis point gap between base and liquidity-adjusted ES is the part I keep watching, because it tells you how much of the firm's apparent risk tolerance depends on the assumption that positions can be exited cleanly and quickly.

The volatility regime is flagged as stressed today, so the model is pulling calibration from a high-vol historical window rather than the recent sample. That choice is mechanical under FRTB, but it matters practically: capital requirements scale with the stressed ES, not the base figure, so the 2.53% number is the operative one for IMA capital purposes.

EEM dropped 6.33% on the day, which is the dominant single-factor contributor to today's ES reading. EM equity drawdowns of that magnitude tend to arrive with currency and credit spread co-movement, so the diversification credit embedded in the portfolio-level ES may be thinner than it appears.

The weekly backtest failed. One VaR exception is not alarming on its own, but the Acerbi-Szekely statistic sitting at -0.4004 suggests the model is systematically underweighting tail severity, not just catching a single outlier. I want to run a cleaner attribution next week to see whether EEM is the primary culprit or if other positions are quietly contributing.
