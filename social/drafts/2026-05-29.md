Quick weekly update on the FRTB Risk Monitor, data as of 2026-05-29:

The headline number today is a 97.5% Expected Shortfall (basically the average loss you'd expect on the worst days, not just the single worst-case cutoff) of 1.56%. Think of that as roughly the cushion a bank running this portfolio would need to hold against a bad day.

Once you layer in the stressed ES (recalibrated to a turbulent historical window, like 2008 or early 2020, to stress-test the model harder), that jumps to 2.47%. Then the liquidity-adjusted figure (which adds extra holding-period penalties for assets that are harder to sell quickly) comes out to 3.36%. So the gap from base to liquidity-adjusted is meaningful, which tells me illiquidity risk is a real driver here, not just noise.

The model is currently flagging a stressed volatility regime, which means recent realized vol is elevated enough that the system is treating conditions more like a crisis period than a calm one. That tends to push capital requirements higher across the board.

Biggest mover today was GLD up about 1.05%. Gold running while everything else is jittery is a pretty classic flight-to-safety signal worth watching.

On the backtest side, zero VaR exceptions and a clean Acerbi-Szekely stat, so the model's loss predictions are holding up well against actual outcomes.

Curious to see if GLD keeps leading next week or gives back gains if sentiment stabilizes.
